If you love food and you love cats here is the perfect place to grab a bite to eat. At Leeds Kitty Café you can enjoy some food and drinks whilst relaxing with the cats who live at the café. The cats have been rescued and are looking to be rehomed and so you could love them for the hour you are there or forever. There is a welfare charge of £6 per adult and £4 for children which helps towards the upkeep of the cats. Booking is essential as it is often booked up in advance. 





This secret Independent bar has been well known for its cocktails for the last few years has now opened a restaurant on the second floor. Set in the American prohibition period of the 1920’ this places oozes charm and sophistication. It has Mediterranean inspired menu with plenty of vegetarian options. With a vast selection of mouthwatering cocktails you will be able to find something to accompany your meal. You will have to keep your eyes peeled for a black door on the street as there are no signs on the street to tell you where it is. 




Originally the main library reading room and then a sculpture court when Leeds Art Gallery opened, the magnificent Victorian Tiled Hall was renovated extensively in 2007. With the help of English Heritage, we revealed the original fabric of the room. Now the space is one of the most popular and iconic eateries in the city of Leeds. The Tiled Hall Café is a distinctive and spacious environment with striking marble columns, stunning ornate tiles and a barrel vaulted mosaic ceiling. Serving upspecially selected hot and cold drinks, fresh salads, sandwiches, hot Panini’s, soup and Jacket potatoes. Vegetarian options, gluten free and dairy-free are all offered. You’re bound to find a treat to suit you.  





The Man Behind The Curtain is a restaurant in Leeds, which was opened in 2014 by chef Michael O'Hare. The restaurant has a reputation for modern and often unusual food, with modern and artistic presentation. It was awarded a Michelin star in October 2015 and three AA Rosettes in 2016. You need to book well ahead as weekend dinner reservations are typically booked six months in advance and for three months in advance for week nights.








You can take away or eat in at the communal table in this Ottoman barbeque cafe. Inspired by Caravanserais of the Middle East, which supported the flow of daily commerce along the ancient trade routes of Silk Road the café is decked out like a caravan and gorgeous rugs adorn the walls inside.  









AKBAR’S as The King of Curry, offers a variety of Indian food ranging from traditional and modern food. Great size naans and tasty curry's a must try. Taste is like home cooked so for curry lovers it's the place to be. The service is top notch and staff is attentive. If you want good tasty, wholesome Asian food, there isn't a better place to go. 






In Leeds historic Kirkgate market you can try a truly Yorkshire product. Locally sourced ingredients all supplied from quality local butchers wrapped in a Yorkshire pudding. There is no need to book as there is a large food court in the market where you can try many different specialties of food. Kirkgate Market was opened on 1 July 1904 by GW Balfour and is one of the largest in Europe. There are 600 stalls and 200,000 people visit the market each week. Michael Marks opened his Penny Bazaar in the original outside market in 1884, which led to the founding of Marks & Spencer in 1890. 



Where else would you get a to see a tuk tuk in the middle of Leeds? Opening its doors in 2015 in their hometown it has proved so popular other towns across the country now have their very own Zaap Thai. When you step inside a Zaap restaurant you will feel like you have been transported into a Bangkok street food market, with all its magical hustle and bustle, neon lighting, funky tuk tuks and, of course the most amazing Thai street food you will ever taste! With over 80 of the most authentic Thai street food dishes on the menu, your taste buds will rock to the Bangkok vibe and you’ll feel like you are in the street food capital of the world, Thailand.
